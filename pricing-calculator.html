How the quote is built

The calculation is the one professional decorators use, made explicit: hard costs (balloons at your per-unit price, plus strips, hooks, frames, and delivery), plus your labor at an honest hourly rate, plus a profit margin on top. The margin is not greed — it covers the unbillable parts of running a decorating side business: quoting time, shopping trips, popped inventory, tools that wear out, and the jobs that never book.

The most common pricing mistake is charging for materials and forgetting the hours. A 120-balloon organic garland takes real time to inflate, cluster, and install — if the quote doesn't include labor, the "profit" is often below minimum wage once you divide by hours worked. That's exactly what the effective earnings-per-hour figure shows you.

Rules of thumb to sanity-check your number

  • Many decorators price organic garlands in the range of $10–$20+ per linear foot depending on region, fullness, and installation complexity — compare that to your quote divided by your garland length.
  • A common shortcut is charging 3× the cost of materials; the full breakdown above is more accurate because it prices your time explicitly rather than hoping the multiple covers it.
  • Quote higher for on-site installs, stairs and ladders, outdoor conditions, and rush jobs. The hours field is where that shows up.
